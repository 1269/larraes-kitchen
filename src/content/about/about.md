heritageNarrative: |
  Chef Larry Cowling grew up in his grandmother Larrae's kitchen in southern Louisiana, where
  Sunday suppers were a two-day affair — gumbo starting Friday afternoon, cornbread cooling on
  the counter, the whole house smelling of onion, bay leaf, and caramelized sugar. Recipes
  weren't written down; they were passed across a countertop, measured by hand, tasted for truth.

  He carried those recipes with him when he moved to Benicia, and he's been cooking them for
  his neighbors ever since. Larrae's Kitchen — named for the grandmother who taught him — isn't
  a trend and it isn't fusion. It's soul food done the way it was taught: slow-braised, seasoned
  by memory, generous with portions, and served exactly the way Larrae would have served it on
  a Sunday afternoon. Every plate that leaves his kitchen is a direct line to the hands that
  taught him how to cook.

  He caters small family gatherings, backyard celebrations, and corporate lunches across the
  greater East Bay. If you're feeding people you love, he'd be honored to help.
