heritageNarrative: |
  Larrae grew up in her grandmother's kitchen in southern Louisiana, where Sunday suppers were a
  two-day affair — gumbo starting Friday afternoon, cornbread cooling on the counter, the whole
  house smelling of onion, bay leaf, and caramelized sugar. Recipes weren't written down; they
  were passed across a countertop, measured by hand, tasted for truth.

  She carried those recipes with her when she moved to Benicia, and she's been cooking them for
  her neighbors ever since. Larrae's Kitchen isn't a trend and it isn't fusion — it's soul food
  done the way it was taught: slow-braised, seasoned by memory, generous with portions, and
  served exactly the way her grandmother would have served it on a Sunday afternoon. Every plate
  that leaves her kitchen is a direct line to the hands that taught her how to cook.

  She caters small family gatherings, backyard celebrations, and corporate lunches across the
  greater East Bay. If you're feeding people you love, she'd be honored to help.
